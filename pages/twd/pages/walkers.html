WALKERS

--The base of The Walking Dead is that the world is undergoing a zombie apocolypse. However, neither the show nor comic books have ever referenced these beings as "Zombies". Here are some of the more famous scenes involving "Walkers"--

Zombies, mostly referred to in-universe as walkers, roamers, lurkers, biters and infected, are an antagonistic force that serve as the primary catalyst for the events within The Walking Dead universe. They serve as universal antagonists. Reanimated human beings, while not immortal, will not "die" under typical conditions that would ordinarily cause the death of a living person. They do not appear to feel or respond to pain, can survive even the most brutal injuries, and despite their bottomless appetite for flesh, they do not need food, water, or sleep to survive. They show no other bodily function that relates to a human, showing no signs of self-healing or response to extreme temperatures. The brain maintains limited abilities of the body, allowing for movement of the limbs (provided that they are not decomposed to the point where the bones are not strong enough to bend without breaking), jaws, neck, and even the use of its sensory systems. While the walkers are notoriously weaker than humans, the only way to kill one is to destroy the brain. Despite severely weakened frames, they will continue to hunt for living animals to consume. Even when decapitated, the head will remain active, even though it would be practically harmless at such point.

Rick realizes that the people who are diseased are in fact no longer alive and he should not think twice about killing them. He does, however still have humanity left in him and apologizes for the horrific trauma the person who died underwent. Meanwhile, Morgan tries to put down his late wife. He can't bring himself to do it.

Inside The Well Walker Scene

Behind the scenes of how the crew of The Walking Dead created 'the well walker', a zombie that somehow ended up at the bottom of the well on the Greene Family Farm. It's interesting to see how much creativity and intuitiveness goes into making the shots so gruesome and repulsing.

Rick and Glenn Disguise Themselves as Walkers

In order to get vehicles to bring them to safety, Rick and Glenn must travel through a horde of walkers without getting noticed. Unfortunately, a walker is able to detect a living being not only by sight, but by sound and smell as well. To escape, they covered themselves in human remains to mask their living smell. Will they make it out?

Rick and Michonne Mow Down Hundreds of Walkers

A herd is approaching the group and they have little time to escape. At the same time, they need to collect explosives that they found on the road in order to fight Negan and the Saviors.

The Biggest Herd

In season 6, the group finds possibly the biggest herd of walkers they have ever encountered. They must safely lead them away from Alexandria, the safezone they live in, or else they could invade.

The Battle for Alexandria

When the herd of walkers makes it's way through the walls of Alexandria, the group must fight and eliminate all of them to live. They don't have a choice. This scene shows just how united the whole group is and how when they work together, they can accomplish great things.

Rick Tries to get Oceanside's Guns

In order to combat Negan and The Saviors, Rick's group needs more firepower. A small secluded community that Tara came upon has the weapons they need. When Rick comes to ask, they are hesitant, but after a herd of walkers shows up, they fight together and trust is formed.

Pushing Walkers Off the Road

The group is fatigued and exhausted from being on the move 24/7. They dont have any vehicles, food or water to spare. On their trail is a growing herd of walkers that they are all too weak to take on hand to hand. They find a nice drop off that they can lure the walkers into in order to thin the herd enough to fight them.

Carol and Daryl in the Van

When Carol and Daryl try to find Beth, they find themselves in the middle of a goup of walkers on a bridge. Their only place of shelter is a nearby van that is hanging off the side of the bridge. They have no choice but to buckle up and hold on as they fall to their possible doom.
